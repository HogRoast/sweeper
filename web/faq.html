What is Sweeper?

Sweeper is a football prediction algorithm, it uses historical match data to generate a statistical analysis of team performance which it then uses to predict results of future fixtures.

Sweeper also provides a general form guide and data analysis of all the upcoming fixtures.

It's all clever stuff!

Well, that is up to you!

Sweeper's main aim is to provide a season-long prediction strategy to maximise potential profit with minimal risk.

In addition, Sweeper provides more general form guides and data analysis which may help you put together your weekly accumulators, or assist with your other football betting strategies.

Why does Sweeper only appear to predict home wins?

This is because statistically and historically speaking, for a football match between any two teams, a home win is a far more likely result than either a draw or an away win.

As an example, over the last 8 seasons in the English Premier League the home team has won roughly 46% of the time.

Is your algorithm back-tested?

Yes it is! Alongside the historical match result data we also have the betting odds that were available at the time of the historical match. This enables Sweeper to simulate bets and determine a paper profit or loss.

Sweeper is configured according to the best P&L result from this back-test process.

Obviously historical performance is not always a perfect guide to future performance.

Why does Sweeper insist on minimum odds for its predicted matches?

Sweeper is a statistics-based algorithm. It is aiming to find the best value bets given the data it has to hand.

So, if a match is being priced by the bookmakers at odds that are less than Sweeper requires, it is not statistically a good bet. This is despite the fact that it might still end up as the correct result.

Help! I haven't won for a few weeks!

This is the nature of football result prediction; sometimes you'll go for weeks getting everything right, and sometimes you won't.

The point is, over the course of an entire season, Sweeper should be right often enough to show a positive return.
